Design Phase - CSRMC

Overview

The Design Phase is the foundation of the CSRMC lifecycle, where cybersecurity and resilience are embedded from the very beginning of system development.

Key Principles

  • Early Stakeholder Engagement: Involves Mission Owner, System Owner, Engineers, and Cybersecurity Service Providers from day one
  • Security by Design: Cybersecurity requirements are incorporated at the inception, not as an afterthought
  • Operational Survivability: Systems are designed to maintain functionality even under cyber attack
  • Threat Modeling: Identifies potential threats and vulnerabilities during the design stage

Benefits

  • Reduces costly security retrofits later in the lifecycle
  • Ensures mission requirements align with security capabilities
  • Creates a foundation for automation and continuous monitoring
  • Improves overall system resilience and cyber survivability
Source:
U.S. Department of War, "Cybersecurity Risk Management Construct," September 2025
https://www.war.gov/News/Releases/Release/Article/4314411/

Build Phase - CSRMC

Overview

The Build Phase transforms secure designs into functional systems, implementing security controls and achieving Initial Operating Capability (IOC).

DevSecOps Integration

  • Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD): Security testing automated in deployment pipelines
  • Infrastructure as Code: Security configurations managed through version control
  • Automated Security Testing: SAST, DAST, and dependency scanning integrated into build process
  • Container Security: Image scanning and runtime protection

Critical Controls Implementation

  • Focus on the most essential cybersecurity protections
  • Address exploitable vulnerabilities identified during design
  • Implement least privilege access controls
  • Enable comprehensive logging and monitoring capabilities
Source:
U.S. Department of War, "DoD CIO Cyber Security Risk Management Construct," September 2025
https://media.defense.gov/2025/Sep/24/2003808112/DOD-CIO-CYBER-SECURITY-RISK-MANAGEMENT-CONSTRUCT.PDF

Test Phase - CSRMC

Overview

Comprehensive security validation through threat-informed testing before achieving Full Operating Capability (FOC).

Testing Activities

  • Penetration Testing: Simulated attacks to identify exploitable vulnerabilities
  • Vulnerability Assessment: Automated and manual scanning for security weaknesses
  • Red Team Exercises: Adversarial emulation to test defensive capabilities
  • Control Validation: Verification that implemented controls function as designed

Threat-Informed Approach

  • Testing based on actual threat actor tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs)
  • Validation against known indicators of compromise (IOCs)
  • Assessment of detection and response capabilities
  • Evaluation of cyber survivability under attack scenarios
Sources:
SecureStrux, "The DoW's New Approach to Cybersecurity Risk Management," 2025

Onboard Phase - CSRMC

Overview

Systems are fully integrated into the operational environment with automated monitoring and continuous authorization to operate (ATO).

Enterprise Integration

  • Enterprise Services: Leverage centralized security services and inherit existing controls
  • Reciprocity: Reuse security assessments and authorizations across systems
  • Automated Onboarding: Streamlined integration process reducing manual effort
  • Continuous ATO: Real-time authorization based on ongoing monitoring

Operational Visibility

  • Dashboard integration providing commanders real-time risk posture
  • Automated threat detection and alerting
  • Active defense capabilities enabled immediately
  • Continuous compliance monitoring and reporting

Key Difference from RMF

Unlike the traditional RMF "snapshot in time" authorization, CSRMC provides continuous authorization based on real-time monitoring and automated controls validation.

Sources:
ExecutiveGov, "Pentagon Implements Cybersecurity Risk Management Construct," 2025

Operations Phase - CSRMC

Overview

Continuous, real-time monitoring with dashboard alerts, rapid threat detection, and immediate response capabilities throughout the operational lifecycle.

Continuous Monitoring

  • Real-Time Dashboards: Immediate visibility into cybersecurity posture for commanders
  • Automated Threat Detection: AI/ML-powered identification of anomalies and threats
  • Correlation and Analysis: Integration of multiple data sources for comprehensive threat intelligence
  • Behavioral Analytics: Detection of insider threats and advanced persistent threats (APTs)

Rapid Response

  • High-risk scenarios trigger immediate automated mitigation actions
  • Playbook-driven incident response for consistent handling
  • Coordination with Security Operations Center (SOC) and incident response teams
  • Rapid patching and remediation workflows

Cyber Survivability

  • Systems continue to function even under active cyber attack
  • Graceful degradation rather than complete failure
  • Mission assurance through defense-in-depth architecture
  • Resilient operations across all domains (air, land, sea, space, cyberspace)

CSRMC Ten Strategic Tenets

1. Automation

Scales operations and eliminates manual errors. Enables routine security tasks and reporting to be handled by automated tools, freeing personnel for strategic activities.

2. Critical Controls

Emphasis on the most essential cybersecurity protections, especially those addressing exploitable vulnerabilities. Focus on controls that provide maximum security value.

3. Continuous Monitoring and Constant ATO

Real-time situational awareness with perpetual system authorization, avoiding lengthy approval cycles and providing commanders with immediate risk visibility.

4. DevSecOps

Integrated security in software development and deployment for agility and resilience. Security is not a gate, but a continuous process throughout the development lifecycle.

5. Cyber Survivability

Systems must continue to function even when under attack. Design for graceful degradation and mission assurance in contested cyber environments.

6. Training

Ensures personnel are equipped to handle new cyber threats and processes. Continuous education and skill development for evolving threat landscape.

7. Enterprise Services & Inheritance

Reduces duplication by reusing controls and previously authorized services. Leverage enterprise-wide security capabilities and assessments.

8. Operationalization

Delivers near real-time visibility of risk posture to commanders and leaders. Security data is actionable intelligence for operational decisions.

9. Reciprocity

Allows reuse of security assessments and authorizations across different systems. Reduces redundant testing and accelerates deployment timelines.

10. Cybersecurity/Threat-Informed Assessments

Validation through active, threat-based testing. Use real-world adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) to validate defensive capabilities.
